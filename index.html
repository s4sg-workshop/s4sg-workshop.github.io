Speech for Social Good Workshop

Despite the fact that speech is the most natural form of human communication, the appeal of speech technologies has been inhibited by their limited availability and accessibility. Globally, speech technologies, including recognition and generation, are concentrated in regions of relative economic stability, which naturally excludes low resource languages from mainstream research. Even where available, these systems have biases in gender and accent, and are mostly unusable for individuals with speech impediments. As speech technologies continue to be employed in an increasing number of application areas - despite the aforementioned shortcomings - we aim to highlight research that will make speech technologies more inclusive and useful for all, and believe that this workshop will provide a platform to address and highlight the social problems of incumbent speech systems, and present new applications of speech systems for social good.

Topics of Interest:

  1. Bias in Speech Technologies - Uncovering and mitigating gender, racial, and other biases in recognition or generation systems,
  2. Impaired Speech - Developing better ASR, learning aids, and diagnostic tools for individuals with speech impediments
  3. ASR for Covid-19 - ASR techniques aimed at recognising speech patterns indicative of Covid-19 symptoms such as coughing, shortness of breath, etc.,
  4. Support for seniors - synthesis and dialog systems that address the unique challenges brought on by age,
  5. Improving speech intelligibility - better synthesis systems for hearing-impaired individuals,
  6. Low Resource Languages - Developing data efficient systems for low resource languages,
  7. Affective computing - ASR systems for emotion recognition, and synthesis/dialog systems for positive human-computer interaction,
